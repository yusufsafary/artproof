Chapter 01

THE PROBLEM

Street art is one of the most significant art movements of the last century, yet it remains one of the most poorly documented. An estimated 97% of street art is painted over, destroyed, or deteriorated within five years of creation — and with it, all record of the artist, the context, and the cultural moment it represented.

Existing documentation tools — social media, photography archives, academic databases — are fragmented, unverified, and inaccessible. Artists have no reliable way to claim their work. Collectors have no way to verify authenticity. Cities have no way to build coherent cultural records.

THE GAP IN THE MARKET

  • No standardized metadata schema for street art documentation
  • No open, verifiable attribution system for artists
  • No protocol connecting physical artwork to a permanent digital record
  • No mechanism for artists to earn from commercial use of their documented work

ArtProof exists to close this gap. We are not a social media platform. We are not a gallery. We are infrastructure for the permanent, verifiable documentation of public art.

Chapter 02

PROTOCOL DESIGN

The ArtProof Protocol (APP) is an open standard for capturing, encoding, and storing verifiable records of street art. It operates in three layers: the Capture Layer, the Verification Layer, and the Archive Layer.

CAPTURE LAYER

Every submission begins with a Capture Event — a structured data packet containing imagery, GPS coordinates, timestamp, device signature, and optional artist declaration. Capture Events are cryptographically signed at point of creation to prevent tampering.

METADATA SCHEMA (AP-META v1)

  • Artwork ID: SHA-256 hash of image + coordinates + timestamp
  • Location: Lat/Long + street address + what3words reference
  • Timestamp: Unix timestamp + UTC offset
  • Capture device: Device fingerprint (anonymized)
  • Artist declaration: Optional signed claim with wallet address
  • Style tags: AI-generated taxonomy (medium, style, scale)

The AP-META schema is compatible with existing cultural heritage standards including Dublin Core, LIDO, and IIIF manifests.

Chapter 03

VERIFICATION LAYER

Verification is the core innovation of ArtProof. We combine AI-driven analysis with community-based consensus to produce a confidence score for every submitted artwork.

AP-AI ENGINE

Our computer vision model is trained on a corpus of 2.4 million verified street art images spanning 140 cities. It performs style clustering, artist fingerprint matching, duplicate detection, surface classification, and cultural context tagging.

COMMUNITY VALIDATION

  • Any verified ArtProof user can review pending submissions
  • Validators stake reputation points on their decisions
  • Disputed attributions enter a 72-hour public review window
  • A supermajority of 67% is required to finalize attribution
  • Validators earn PROOF tokens for accurate consensus contributions

This hybrid approach ensures AI efficiency is balanced by human judgment — particularly important for anonymous or pseudonymous artists where AI fingerprinting alone is insufficient.

Chapter 04

TOKENOMICS

The PROOF token is the economic backbone of the ArtProof ecosystem. It is a utility token designed to incentivize accurate documentation and fair attribution — not speculation.

TOKEN DISTRIBUTION

Chapter 05

GOVERNANCE & DAO

ArtProof is governed by the ArtProof DAO — in which PROOF token holders collectively make decisions about protocol development, fee structures, grant allocations, and partnership approvals.

GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

  • Any holder of 1,000+ PROOF can submit a governance proposal
  • Proposals pass with 60% approval and minimum 10% quorum
  • Core protocol changes require 75% supermajority
  • Emergency proposals can be fast-tracked via Security Council

TRANSITION TIMELINE

ArtProof transitions from core team governance to full DAO governance over 24 months. Phase 1 (0-12 months): Core team retains veto rights. Phase 2 (12-24 months): Graduated power transfer. Phase 3 (24+ months): Full DAO autonomy.

What exactly is ArtProof?
+
ArtProof is an open protocol for verifying, archiving, and attributing street art. Think of it as a permanent, tamper-proof public record for urban art — combining AI verification, community consensus, and on-chain documentation.
Is ArtProof free to use?
+
The core documentation and archive features are free forever. Premium features — advanced API access, institutional integrations, and commercial licensing — are available through paid tiers and PROOF token usage.
Does ArtProof encourage illegal graffiti?
+
No. ArtProof documents what exists — it does not advocate for any particular form of art-making. We work with city governments and property owners as partners, and our community guidelines explicitly prohibit using the platform to coordinate illegal activity. Documentation is neutral; it is the act of witnessing, not endorsing.
What happens to artworks that get painted over?
+
This is precisely why ArtProof exists. Once documented and verified, an artwork's record is permanent — even if the physical piece is destroyed. The archive entry is immutable, the artist attribution is preserved, and the cultural moment is not lost.
How do I claim my artwork on ArtProof?
+
Once your work is submitted and enters the verification queue, you can file an Artist Claim through your profile. You'll need to provide supporting evidence — process photos, sketches, or a signed declaration. Claims are reviewed by the community within 72 hours.
What if I want to stay anonymous?
+
Anonymity is fully supported. You can operate under a pseudonym and connect your claim to a wallet address rather than a legal identity. Your on-chain record is permanent and tied to your pseudonymous identity.
How do royalties work for artists?
+
When your verified artwork is used commercially — in publications, advertising, or licensed reproductions — ArtProof's licensing protocol triggers an automatic royalty payment to your wallet in PROOF tokens. The standard rate is 15% of the licensing fee, adjustable by DAO governance.
Can I remove my work from the archive?
+
Artists who have claimed their work can apply for a Suppression Order — which hides the artwork from public search results while preserving the verification record. Full deletion is not possible given the on-chain nature of the archive.
How do I become a validator?
+
Validators must complete a 2-hour onboarding course covering art history basics, the AP-META schema, and community guidelines. Upon completion, you receive Validator status. Early validators during Phase 3 receive a founding bonus of 500 PROOF tokens.
What happens if I validate incorrectly?
+
Validators stake reputation points on each decision. Incorrect validations result in reputation loss. Repeated poor validation can lead to temporary suspension. This system ensures high accuracy while remaining fair to honest mistakes.
How much can validators earn?
+
Active validators completing 20+ reviews per week with 95%+ accuracy can earn between 200-800 PROOF tokens monthly in the current emission period. Token value fluctuates with market conditions.
Which blockchain does ArtProof use?
+
ArtProof is built on Solana for high-speed, low-cost on-chain documentation and governance, paired with Arweave for permanent data storage. Solana's throughput ensures real-time verification at scale. We are exploring multi-chain expansion in Phase 5.

Is the protocol open source?
+
Yes. The AP-META schema, verification contracts, and archive indexer are all open source under MIT license. The AI model weights are currently proprietary but will be released under a research license in Phase 5. All smart contracts are audited and publicly verifiable.
How does the AI detect duplicate submissions?
+
Our duplicate detection uses perceptual hashing combined with GPS radius filtering. New submissions are compared against all existing entries within a 50-meter radius. Similarity above 85% triggers a manual review flag before the submission enters the standard verification queue.
